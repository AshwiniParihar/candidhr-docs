## Team Members

The **Users** tab lets you invite new team members, manage their access levels, and monitor account activity. Navigate to **Profile → Users** to get started.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/settings/image7.png" alt="Team Members list showing active users" />
</Frame>

<Note>
  Only users with sufficient permissions (typically Admins) can invite users, modify roles, or manage user access.
</Note>

---

## Inviting a New User

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/settings/image8.png" alt="Invite user popup" />
</Frame>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Invite popup">
    Click **Invite User** in the top-right corner of the Users tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the details">
    | Field | Description | Example |
    |---|---|---|
    | Full Name | Official name of the team member | Diana Prince |
    | Email Address | Where the invitation email will be sent | diana@company.com |
    | Role | Determines permissions and access level | Admin / Editor / Participant |
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send the invitation">
    Click **Send Invite**. The user receives an email, clicks the link, and creates their account. Their account becomes active after setup is complete.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Members & Invites Tabs

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/settings/image9.png" alt="Members and Invites tabs" />
</Frame>

| Tab | Description |
|---|---|
| Members | Active users with name, email, status, role, last login, and date added |
| Invites | Invitation records: Pending, Accepted, Expired, or Revoked |

### Filters & Sorting

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/settings/image10.png" alt="Filters and sorting options" />
</Frame>

| Option | Description |
|---|---|
| All Members | Complete overview of every team member |
| Admin / Editor / Participant | Filter by role |
| Pending / Accepted / Expired / Revoked | Filter invitations by status |
| Sort Options | Date Added, Name (A–Z), or Last Login |

**Best Practices**
- Assign users only the permissions necessary for their responsibilities
- Regularly review inactive or unused user accounts
- Use official business email addresses when inviting users
- Monitor pending and expired invitations regularly

## Email & Communication Templates

Create, manage, and reuse communication templates for candidate outreach. CandidHR includes two pre-built templates — **Shortlisted Candidate** and **Rejected Applicant** — and you can create additional ones as needed.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/settings/image11.png" alt="Templates management page" />
</Frame>

| Field | Options / Description |
|---|---|
| Status | Shortlist, Reject, On Hold, Hire, General, or Schedule Final Interview |
| Category | Organises templates by purpose |
| Visibility | Private (creator only) or Global (all organisation users) |
| Note | The actual communication content |

### Creating a Template

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Templates">
    Navigate to **Profile → Templates** and click **Create Template**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Status">
    Choose the appropriate status (e.g. Shortlist, Reject, On Hold).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Category">
    Select a category to organise the template.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Visibility">
    Set to **Private** (only you) or **Global** (accessible to all organisation users).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter content and save">
    Enter the communication content in the **Note** field and click **Save**. The template is now available in recruitment workflows.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Global templates are accessible to all users within the organisation, while Private templates are visible only to the creator.
</Tip>

**Best Practices**
- Maintain professional and consistent communication across all templates
- Use categories to organise templates effectively
- Regularly review and update templates to align with hiring workflows
- Use Global templates for organisation-wide communication standards
