## Overview

Creating a job in CandidHR takes you through a wizard with four steps:
1. **Basic Info** — title, department, location, description
2. **Preferences** — skills, experience, education, and other criteria
3. **Questions** — optional custom questions for the application form
4. **Workflow** — which screening stages to include

---

## Step-by-step

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Jobs">
    Click **Jobs** in the left sidebar. You'll land on the jobs list page.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click + New Job">
    Click the **+ New Job** button in the top-right corner of the jobs list. This opens the job creation wizard.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Fill in Basic Info">
    Complete the following fields:

    | Field | Required | Notes |
    |---|---|---|
    | Job Title | Yes | e.g. "Senior Frontend Engineer" |
    | Department | No | e.g. "Engineering" |
    | Job Type | Yes | Full-time, Part-time, Contract, etc. |
    | Location | Yes | City, or "Remote" |
    | Salary Range | No | Optional. Shown on the public job page. |
    | Job Description | Yes | Supports rich text — bullets, bold, headings. |

    Click **Next** to proceed.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set Screening Preferences">
    Preferences define the criteria CandidHR uses to automatically score resumes.

    **Skills**
    - Add required skills and assign weights (e.g., React: 30%, Node.js: 20%)
    - Skills with higher weight have more impact on the screening score

    **Experience**
    - Set a minimum and maximum years of experience range
    - Candidates outside this range are flagged

    **Education**
    - Set a minimum qualification (e.g., Bachelor's degree)

    **Other criteria**
    - English proficiency level
    - Notice period
    - Current location preference

    <Tip>
      You can save preferences as a template and reuse them for similar roles.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add Custom Questions (Optional)">
    Add questions that candidates must answer during the application form.

    - **Text** — free-form answer
    - **Multiple choice** — single or multi-select options
    - **Yes/No** — simple boolean question
    - **File upload** — ask for a portfolio, cover letter, etc.

    You can mark any question as **Required** or **Knockout** (a wrong answer disqualifies the candidate automatically).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Assign a Workflow">
    A workflow defines the stages of your hiring pipeline for this job.

    - Choose an **existing workflow** from the dropdown
    - Or click **Create New Workflow** to build one from scratch

    See [Build a Workflow](/jobs/build-a-workflow) for a full guide.

    <Note>
      You can change the workflow after creating the job, as long as no candidates have progressed past the first stage.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save as Draft or Launch">
    At the end of the wizard:
    - Click **Save as Draft** to continue editing later
    - Click **Launch Job** to make it live immediately

    A launched job generates a **public application link** you can share with candidates.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Editing a job after creation

You can edit any job detail at any time:

1. Go to **Jobs** → click the job title
2. Click the **Edit** button on the job overview page
3. Make your changes and save

<Warning>
  Changing the job description or preferences after candidates have applied will not retroactively change their screening scores. Re-score from the Resume Screening tab if needed.
</Warning>

---

## Duplicating a job

To reuse a job setup for a similar role:

1. Open the job from the Jobs list
2. Click the **...** (more options) menu
3. Select **Duplicate Job**

This copies all settings (preferences, questions, workflow assignment) into a new draft job.
